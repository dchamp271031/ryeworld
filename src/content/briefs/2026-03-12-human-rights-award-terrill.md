# Autumn Terrill Wins 2026 Rye Human Rights Award for Employing Disabled Adults

## What happened / What's coming
Autumn Terrill, founder of Give Good Parties, Inc., won the 2026 Rye Human Rights Award from the Rye City Human Rights Commission. The award was announced at the March 4 City Council meeting. Give Good Parties, launched in September 2025, is an event planning company that employs differently-abled adults, providing paid 1:1 job training. Terrill was inspired by her brother Adam, who has autism and whom she advocated to adopt at age 5. The company now has nearly 10 employees and is gearing up for graduation season.

## Why it matters to Rye
This is Rye's highest recognition for human rights work, and it went to a business that's actively creating employment opportunities for disabled adults right here in town. Terrill lives off Forrest Avenue. Her model of connecting employees with local businesses and nonprofits through event planning is community building in the purest sense.

- Who: Autumn Terrill, Give Good Parties, Inc.; nominated by Laura Labriola and Rita Capek
