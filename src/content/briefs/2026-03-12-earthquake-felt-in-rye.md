# 2.3-Magnitude Earthquake Felt in Rye, No Damage Reported

## What happened / What's coming
A 2.3-magnitude earthquake struck west of Sleepy Hollow at 10:17 AM on Tuesday, March 10, 2026. Over 100 people in the Rye area reported feeling light shaking. City officials confirmed no damage. Westchester County Emergency Services found no infrastructure damage or operational impacts. Officials at the decommissioned Indian Point nuclear site conducted precautionary safety surveys and reported no issues.

## Why it matters to Rye
Earthquakes are rare and memorable in Westchester. More than 100 Rye-area residents felt this one. County Executive Ken Jenkins used the event to argue against proposals to reopen Indian Point, saying seismic activity underscores why a nuclear plant shouldn't operate in Westchester. This is a conversation starter at every dinner table in town.
