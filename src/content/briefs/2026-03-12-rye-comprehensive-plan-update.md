# Rye Approves $285K Contract to Rewrite Its 40-Year-Old Comprehensive Plan

## What happened / What's coming
The Rye City Council voted to approve a $285,000 contract with AKRF, a planning and environmental consulting firm, to develop a brand-new comprehensive plan for the city. Rye's current plan dates back to 1985 and has never been updated. AKRF's team includes OLIN Studio (parks and open space), Barton & Loguidice (transportation and infrastructure), and the Pace University Land Use Law Center (public engagement). The project is expected to run 18 months through 2027.

## Why it matters to Rye
This will reshape how Rye develops for the next generation. The plan will address land use, housing pressures, climate resiliency, flood mitigation, transportation, and environmental policy. With 14 stakeholder meetings, multiple public workshops, and virtual engagement planned, every resident has a chance to weigh in on Rye's future. The 1985 plan was written before most current residents moved here.

## Key details
- Who: AKRF (Peter Feroe, principal; Corey Block, project manager), OLIN Studio, Barton & Loguidice, Pace Land Use Law Center
- What: New comprehensive plan for Rye replacing the 1985 version
- When: Approved March 4, 2026; estimated completion 2027
- Where: City-wide scope
- How much: $285,000
