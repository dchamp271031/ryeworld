The weather is cooperating for once, and Rye is taking full advantage. Here's what's worth your time this weekend.

Saturday morning kicks off with the early-season farmers market at the Rye Town Park pavilion. It's a smaller setup than the peak summer version, but the vendors who show up in March are the diehards — expect local eggs, winter greens, bread from the good bakeries, and the honey guy who's been there since before the market was popular. Get there by 9 if you want the full selection; by 10:30 the best stuff is gone.

Saturday afternoon, the Rye Arts Center is hosting an open studio event. Drop in between 1 and 4 PM to see work from local artists and catch a ceramics demo. Free admission, kid-friendly, and a genuinely nice way to kill an hour without spending money on Purchase Street for once.

Sunday is the main event for a lot of families: the Rye Nature Center is running its spring trail walk at 10 AM. It's a guided loop through the preserve, about an hour, with a naturalist pointing out what's budding and what's returning. Dress for mud — the trails are soft this time of year and they don't cancel for it.

On the restaurant side, a few spots along Purchase Street are setting up sidewalk tables for the first time this year. No official announcement, just chairs appearing outside like crocuses. If it stays above 55 degrees, expect every two-top to be claimed by noon. Grab one early and pretend summer is already here.
