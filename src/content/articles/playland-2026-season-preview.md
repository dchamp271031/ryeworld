Last summer was rough for Playland. Standard Amusements walked away from its operating contract, the Dragon Coaster sat idle for most of the season, and the whole park had an energy that felt like a party where the host left early. People still showed up — it's Playland, people always show up — but there was a flatness to it that nobody enjoyed pretending wasn't there.

That's over. Westchester County has released the full plan for Playland's 98th season, and the headline is the one everyone's been waiting for: the Dragon Coaster is coming back. The ride's been rebuilt with replacement elements in sturdier materials, which is the kind of unsexy infrastructure detail that matters a lot when you're talking about a wooden coaster that's been running since 1929. It needed the work. It got the work. It'll be ready.

## the dates

Preseason weekends start May 16-17. Grand opening is May 23. The park runs Wednesday through Sunday through September 7. Fourth of July fireworks are confirmed, and weekly Friday night fireworks are returning — which, if you've never watched fireworks over Long Island Sound from the boardwalk, is one of the better free experiences in Westchester.

## what it costs

Admission to the park is free. That's always been the case and it still surprises people. You pay for rides, and the wristband pricing is reasonable: $27 for Westchester residents, $32 for everyone else. Season passes are $85 resident, $100 non-resident — which pays for itself in three visits if your kids are the type who want to ride everything twice.

Parking is $15 per car on regular days, $20 on holidays. Budget for it, don't complain about it, and get there before 11 AM on Saturdays if you want a spot that doesn't require a hike.

## why this year matters

The county stepping in to run things directly after the Standard Amusements situation is either going to be a turning point or a cautionary tale, and season 98 is the test. County Executive Ken Jenkins is putting the park front and center, and the investment in the Dragon Coaster rebuild signals that this isn't a placeholder year. They're trying to get it right.

Playland has always been Rye's backyard — the place where your summer memories stack up whether you plan them or not. A kid's first roller coaster. A terrible date that became a great story. The specific smell of boardwalk food mixed with salt air that hits you the second you walk through the gate.

A good Playland season makes the whole summer feel different. This one has the setup to be exactly that.
