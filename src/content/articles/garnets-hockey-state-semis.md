Fernando Mosquera saw 51 shots on Saturday in Rochester. He stopped 50 of them. And when the final horn sounded, Rye 4, Aquinas 1, the Garnets had punched their ticket to a place this program hasn't been since 2007 — the New York State semifinals.

Let that number sit for a second. Fifty saves. In a quarterfinal. Against a Section 5 champion that had been rolling through its bracket all winter. Mosquera didn't just keep Rye in the game. He was the game. The kind of performance you remember 20 years from now when someone asks you about the year Garnets hockey went to Buffalo.

But this wasn't a goalie carrying a team that had no business being there. This was a complete, ruthless road win by a squad that has been building toward this moment all season. Rye is 17-3-3. They earned the state tournament's only at-large bid after a gut-punch 3-2 overtime loss to Byram Hills in the Section 1 finals — a game that could have ended their season and their story. Instead, it became the prologue.

Jamie Morris scored the go-ahead goal shorthanded. Read that again. Down a man, Morris buried it to give Rye a lead they never gave back. Kai Brittain added a goal and an assist. Colin Nigro converted on the power play. Will Niejadlik set up two goals with the kind of vision that makes linemates look better than they already are. And Ryan Draddy sealed it with an empty-netter that let every Rye parent in that Rochester rink finally exhale.

Head Coach Pete Thomas has built something here. Not overnight, not with a single recruiting class, but through the slow, stubborn work of turning a program into a culture. The Garnets lost in the section finals and could have packed it in. Instead, they regrouped, got the at-large bid, drove to Rochester, and dismantled a team on its home turf.

Now comes Saturday. March 14. LECOM Harborcenter in Buffalo. The state semifinals.

Here's the part that makes this story even better: Byram Hills is also in the Final Four. The team that beat Rye in overtime for the Section 1 title is on the other side of the bracket. If both teams win Saturday, they meet again — in the state championship game. A Section 1 rematch for all the marbles. The kind of storyline you couldn't write if you tried.

But Rye isn't looking ahead. They can't afford to. Saturday's semifinal opponent will be a team that earned its way to Buffalo too, and the Garnets will need Mosquera to be Mosquera, the penalty kill to keep doing its job, and the depth scoring that showed up against Aquinas to show up one more time.

Nineteen years is a long wait. The last time Rye hockey made the state semis, the current roster was negative-three years old. This group doesn't carry that history — they're writing their own. Two wins from a state title. The bus to Buffalo leaves soon. If you can make the drive, make the drive. This team has earned a crowd.
