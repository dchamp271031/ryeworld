The Rye City Council's March 11 agenda included a vote on a $1.47 million Pierce pumper engine for the Rye Fire Department. For a city this size, that's a significant line item.

Fire equipment runs on long replacement cycles. When a pumper reaches end of life, you don't patch it — you buy new. Pierce is one of the top manufacturers in the business, and a pumper at this price point is a front-line engine built to last the department another 15 to 20 years.

## what else was on the agenda

The March 11 meeting wasn't just the fire truck. Council also took up the 2026-2028 Rye Recreation Camp Transportation contract — relevant to every parent sending kids to Rye Rec this summer — along with a Stop DWI Patrol agreement with Westchester County and a snow removal agreement.

The next council meeting is March 25 at City Hall.

## the bigger picture

Capital spending like this doesn't generate the same heat as a zoning fight or a school budget vote, but it adds up. A $1.47M truck is a bet on public safety infrastructure, and it comes out of the same budget that funds everything else the city does.

Worth knowing where your tax dollars go, even when it's not controversial.
