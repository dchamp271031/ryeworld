Purchase Street is a half-mile stretch that punches well above its weight. You could eat your way down one side and back up the other for a week and never repeat a meal. Here's what's actually worth your money.

Start at the north end. Frankie & Johnnie's has been holding it down longer than most of us have had mortgages. The steaks are consistent, the portions are honest, and the old-school Italian-American thing never gets old when it's done right. Two blocks south, Rafele keeps the Italian conversation going but with a Neapolitan edge — the pasta is handmade and the wine list is deep without being pretentious about it.

For something faster, Rye House does a solid burger and keeps the beer list rotating. MP Taverna brings Greek food that actually tastes like someone's grandmother made it, which is the highest compliment you can give a Mediterranean spot. And if you're looking for sushi, Koku is the move — omakase-quality fish in a town that could easily get away with serving mediocre rolls. They don't.

The coffee situation anchors everything. You'll find your morning routine within a week of moving here, and you'll defend it like it's a political position. That's just how Purchase Street works — it turns you into a regular whether you planned on it or not.
