It was 65 degrees on Monday. By Wednesday the forecast is calling for a rain-snow mix and a high of 51 dropping into the 30s. That's March in Rye — the season doesn't arrive so much as it negotiates, showing up for a day, disappearing, then coming back like it never left. But the calendar doesn't lie, and the next two months are stacked.

Here's what's coming and when to show up.

## marshlands conservancy guided spring walk — march 28

The best free thing in Rye that most people forget exists. Marshlands Conservancy (220 Boston Post Road) is running a guided walk on Saturday, March 28 from 10 to 11:30 AM. A naturalist leads you through the preserve pointing out what's returning, what's blooming, and what the early signs of spring actually look like when someone who knows is doing the pointing. No registration fee, no gear required — just shoes you don't mind getting muddy.

Marshlands sits on 147 acres along the Sound and manages to feel like you're nowhere near a commuter town. If you've only ever driven past it on the Post Road, this is your reason to stop.

## rye nature center — ongoing weekends

The Nature Center has been running weekend programs all winter, but the spring slate is when it gets good. Trail walks, family programs, and seasonal events pick up through March and April. Check their calendar for specifics — programs tend to fill, especially the ones geared toward younger kids. The preserve trails are open daily regardless, and right now, before everything leafs out, is one of the best times to walk them. You can actually see the landscape.

## rye farmers market — opens may 3

The Down to Earth Farmers Market returns to Theodore Fremd Avenue on Sunday, May 3. Sundays, 9 AM to 2 PM, running through the fall. The early weeks are the best — vendors are eager, the produce is just starting to turn over from winter storage to actual spring crops, and the crowd hasn't reached peak stroller density yet. Get there closer to 9 than to noon.

## playland preseason — may 16-17

Before the official grand opening on May 23, Playland opens for a preseason weekend on May 16-17. If you want the park without the crowds, this is it. The Dragon Coaster will be back after sitting out most of last season, and Friday night fireworks return for the full summer run.

## the move

If you do one thing this spring before the summer calendar takes over, make it the Marshlands walk on March 28. It's free, it's beautiful, and it's the kind of morning that reminds you why you live here. The farmers market and Playland will be there all summer. Marshlands in early spring, when everything is just starting to wake up, is a narrow window. Don't miss it.
