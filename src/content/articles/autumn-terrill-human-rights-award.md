The story starts with a brother. Adam has autism, and when Autumn Terrill was old enough to understand what that meant for his future — the gaps in employment, the lack of places that would meet him where he was — she didn't just worry about it. She advocated to adopt him when she was five years old. Five. Most of us were learning to tie our shoes.

That instinct never went away. It just got more structured.

Terrill, who lives off Forrest Avenue, launched Give Good Parties in September 2025. The company does event planning — corporate, private, community — but with a model that's built around employing differently-abled adults. Every employee gets paid, 1:1 job training, and real work that actually matters. Not busywork. Not a program that looks good in a brochure. Actual events for actual clients who chose Give Good Parties because the work is good.

The company now has nearly ten employees and is ramping up for graduation season, which is when event planning in Westchester kicks into a different gear entirely. That they're growing this fast, less than two years in, says something about both the demand and the execution.

On March 4, the Rye City Human Rights Commission gave Terrill the 2026 Rye Human Rights Award at the City Council meeting. It's the city's highest recognition for human rights work, and it went to someone who's building the thing she wished existed when she was a kid watching her brother navigate a world that wasn't designed for him.

What makes Give Good Parties worth paying attention to isn't just the mission — it's that the mission is the business model, not a line item in a corporate responsibility report. Terrill figured out that the gap wasn't in willingness. Disabled adults want to work. Businesses need event help. The gap was in someone connecting those two things with proper training and support in between. That's what Give Good Parties does, and it does it locally — partnering with Rye businesses and nonprofits to place employees where they can learn and contribute.

Laura Labriola and Rita Capek nominated Terrill for the award, and it's the kind of nomination that writes itself. The harder thing was building the company in the first place — taking the personal and turning it into something sustainable, repeatable, and growing.

Graduation season is coming. If you're planning an event, you could hire any number of planners in Westchester. Or you could hire the one that's actually changing something. Give Good Parties is the call.
