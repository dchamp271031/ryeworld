Two of seven seats on the Rye City School District Board of Education are on the ballot May 19. Candidate packets become available March 27 at the district's Central Administration Offices at 555 Theodore Fremd Avenue, Suite B-101.

Incumbents Shaun Kloepfer and Tom Stein are both eligible to run again. Whether they do — and who else steps up — will start to become clear over the next few weeks.

## the deadlines

Candidate petitions are due April 29 at 5 PM. That's a hard deadline, not a suggestion. If you've been thinking about running, or know someone who should be, the window is open but not for long.

The next Board of Education meeting is March 24 at 7:30 PM, which is a good place to see how the current board operates before deciding whether you want a seat at the table.

## why it matters

School board races in Rye are never throwaway elections. The district consistently ranks among the best in Westchester, and the board controls decisions on budgets, curriculum, facilities, and staffing that touch every family in the district. Two seats out of seven is enough to shift the direction of a conversation.

Board President Jane Anderson and Vice President Matthew Schibanoff lead the current board. The two open seats will help determine priorities for the 2026-2027 school year and beyond.

Mark the dates: packets March 27, petitions April 29, election May 19.
