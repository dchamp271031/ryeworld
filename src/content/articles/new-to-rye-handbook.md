Welcome. You bought the house, survived the bidding war, and now you're standing in your kitchen wondering where to get a decent cup of coffee. Here's the stuff nobody puts in the welcome packet.

First: parking on Purchase Street on a Saturday is a contact sport. The lot behind the stores is your best bet, but you'll learn the rhythm — there's a sweet spot around 9:15 AM before the brunch crowd but after the early gym people clear out. Don't even think about the meters during the holiday season. Just walk from home if you can.

Second: join things. The Rye Nature Center runs programs for every age group and it's the fastest way to meet people who actually live here year-round. Rye Recreation has sports leagues, swim programs, and summer camps that fill up faster than you'd expect, so mark registration dates on your calendar the day they're announced. The YMCA is another hub — solid gym, good pool, and the kind of place where you'll run into the same people enough times that you stop being strangers.

Third: learn the geography. Playland is for summer weekends, Rye Town Park requires a resident pass (get it early), and Milton Point has the quieter waterfront if you want to skip the crowds. The Square House on Purchase Street is a museum now, but it's also a landmark people use for directions the way New Yorkers use subway stations. Get your bearings around it.

The most important thing: Rye is small enough that your reputation is your currency. Be decent, show up to school events, wave to the crossing guards. You'll feel like a local faster than you think.
