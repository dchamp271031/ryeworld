The sticks are out. The turf at Memorial Field is getting its spring treatment. And if you've driven past the high school after 3 PM this week, you already know — Garnets lacrosse is back in session.

Last year's squad made a deep run into sectionals before a heartbreaker in the semis. The seniors who graduated took a lot of production with them, but what's coming up behind them might be better. The midfield is stacked with juniors who spent last season learning the hard way, and the defensive unit returns three starters who spent the offseason at top club programs. The cage has a legitimate competition brewing between two goalies who both want it badly, which is exactly the kind of problem a coaching staff loves to have.

The schedule doesn't do them any favors. Mamaroneck in week two is a statement game, and the Bronxville matchup in April is circled on every whiteboard in the athletic office. But this group has the kind of quiet confidence that comes from knowing they were close last year and knowing exactly what it cost them to fall short. The home opener is March 22 at Memorial Field. Bring a chair. The bleachers fill up fast once word gets around that this team is for real.
